# Fragmentation risk

Fragmented flexibility initiatives can create attractive local business cases while reducing total system value.

## Pattern

A local initiative may optimise one asset type with one partner and show high gross flexibility value. The calculation boundary may exclude rebound, imbalance, wholesale position impact, cross-asset dependencies and customer-system effects.

## Examples of fragmentation

- EV charging integrated with a limited set of vehicle brands or charging partners.
- Solar and battery solutions built with different partners and separate optimisation logic.
- Flexibility bids sent to ancillary markets without integration to wholesale position management.
- Rebound handled as an operational afterthought.
- Imbalance costs hidden, ignored or attributed elsewhere.
- Assets optimised locally without household residual-load context.
- Energy sharing treated as an administrative issue instead of a customer and portfolio value source.

## Simulator role

The simulator should compare the local gross case with the integrated net case.

It should show how a local solution can look profitable in isolation but become less attractive after system effects are included.
