# Customer value and progression

An integrated energy system should create value for the customer as well as for the portfolio owner.

## Customer value

Customer value can come from:

- lower energy cost,
- better use of local production,
- smarter charging,
- energy sharing between sites,
- comfort and target-state protection,
- flexibility bonus,
- simpler operation,
- better visibility of the household energy system.

## Progression effect

A coherent product can create a progression loop. The customer does not only buy one device; the customer builds an energy system step by step.

Examples:

- adding a cabin battery improves the value of cabin solar,
- replacing a heat pump with a controllable model improves system optimisation,
- adding V2X makes the vehicle part of the portfolio,
- better telemetry improves forecast and value attribution.

The product can show the next-best upgrade and the expected monthly improvement. This can increase customer engagement, retention and willingness to add new assets over time.

## Strategic meaning

The value is not only in one asset. The value is in the coherent system around home, cabin, vehicle, production, storage, heating and market participation.
